# Anti-Drift Rules

How to keep glitchcore from turning into generic cyberpunk sludge, fake glitch filters, dead beauty edits, or random effect soup.

## Purpose

This file defines the major drift risks in glitchcore and how to correct them.

"Drift" means the style starts out interesting, then slowly mutates into something weaker, safer, more generic, or less structurally true.

In glitchcore, drift usually happens when people mistake:
- Effect for logic
- Color for style
- Distortion for meaning
- Trendiness for specificity
- Decoration for transformation

This file is here to prevent that dumb shit.

---

## 1. Core Anti-Drift Principle

**Glitchcore is NOT:**
- Random effects stacked on a portrait
- Generic RGB split
- Cyberpunk makeup
- "Hot girl + neon + blur"
- Low-quality export for its own sake
- Moodboard fluff
- Default black-background sadness
- Retro tech cosplay
- Polished editorial with a tiny corruption garnish

**Glitchcore IS:**
- Artifact-driven identity distortion
- Media-failure logic affecting the body
- Screen behavior acting on anatomy
- Emotional consequence through corruption
- Figure transformation via signal systems
- Intentional palette pressure
- Structured instability

If the image can lose all its glitch behaviors and still basically be the same image, it's probably drifting.

---

## 2. The Main Drift Directions

### DRIFT A — Generic Cyberpunk Editorial

**What it looks like:**
- Clean neon face
- Magenta/cyan lighting
- Fashionable subject
- Sharp glossy finish
- Maybe one chromatic split
- Maybe some UI lines
- Looks expensive and safe

**Why it's wrong:**
This is style-by-association, not artifact logic. The image says "future-ish" but not "media-corrupted body."

**Common symptoms:**
- Too polished
- Too cinematic
- No real image instability
- No visible artifact driver
- Cyberpunk lighting doing all the work

**Fix:**
Add actual corruption:
- Channel misregistration
- Compression chew
- Temporal lag
- Text debris
- Scanline anatomy
- Overprint layers
- Visible media injury

**Replacement thought:**
Don't ask: "Does this look futuristic?"
Ask: "Has the image actually been re-authored by a failure mode?"

---

### DRIFT B — Fake Glitch Filter Bullshit

**What it looks like:**
- One RGB split slapped on top
- Fake VHS lines
- Random displacement
- Stock "glitch effect" plugin energy
- Image underneath untouched

**Why it's wrong:**
The glitch is just a sticker. It doesn't alter identity, movement, emotion, or structure.

**Common symptoms:**
- Face remains pristine
- Artifacts sit only at edges
- Effects feel canned
- Everything too evenly distributed
- No relation between distortion and body language

**Fix:**
Make corruption structural:
- Split the eyes, lips, or silhouette
- Let trails follow real motion
- Let text interfere with facial zones
- Let compression bruise skin gradients
- Let scanlines build anatomy, not just texture the whole frame

**Replacement thought:**
Glitch should not sit on the body. It should get into the body.

---

### DRIFT C — Beauty Edit with a Glitch Accessory

**What it looks like:**
- Stunning glam portrait
- Soft bloom
- Pretty makeup
- Maybe one split-channel edge
- Very Instagram-beautiful
- Damage is timid and decorative

**Why it's wrong:**
It's just beauty imagery wearing glitch as jewelry.

**Common symptoms:**
- No instability
- No emotional weirdness
- Too cleanly seductive
- No real degradation
- Too intentional in a commercial sense

**Fix:**
Ruin something valuable:
- Let lipstick bleed through chroma damage
- Let lashes buzz with compression noise
- Let the mouth duplicate
- Let cheeks band under quantized glow
- Let text cross the face
- Let glamour survive through injury, not avoid it

**Replacement thought:**
Make it hot, then let the file betray it.

---

### DRIFT D — Dead Black Monitor Jail

**What it looks like:**
- Black background
- Cyan face
- Some scanlines
- Minimal signal
- No palette life
- No visual surprise
- Same mood every time

**Why it's wrong:**
This branch is real, but it becomes a prison when treated as the default.

**Common symptoms:**
- Too much empty black
- Too little chromatic action
- Coldness with no tension
- All pieces reading the same
- Visual austerity without payoff

**Fix:**
Reintroduce:
- Accent collisions
- White punctures
- Pop palette branches
- Signal density shifts
- Stronger body retrieval paths
- Toxic candy or gloss-electric variants

**Replacement thought:**
Black is a contrast tool, not a religion.

---

### DRIFT E — Rainbow Clown Soup

**What it looks like:**
- All colors active
- All at high saturation
- No hierarchy
- Lots of effects
- Nothing leads
- Eye gets punched everywhere at once

**Why it's wrong:**
Maximum color without structure becomes visual diarrhea.

**Common symptoms:**
- No dominant hue
- No collision logic
- No anchor region
- Subject drowned in chroma soup
- "Fun" but useless

**Fix:**
Use palette architecture:
- One dominant family
- One collision partner
- One accent intrusion
- Saturation hierarchy
- Brighter and duller regions
- Stable focal area

**Replacement thought:**
Overload needs leadership.

---

### DRIFT F — Random Effect Soup

**What it looks like:**
- Scanlines
- Blur
- RGB split
- Text
- Noise
- Displacement
- Glow
- Blocks
- All present, no logic

**Why it's wrong:**
The image becomes an inventory of tricks, not a coherent signal event.

**Common symptoms:**
- No primary driver
- No layer hierarchy
- Same opacity everywhere
- No emotional specificity
- Eye cannot tell what matters

**Fix:**
Choose:
- 1 primary artifact driver
- 1–2 secondaries
- 0–2 support textures

Then reduce everything else.

**Replacement thought:**
A stack is not a system unless something leads.

---

### DRIFT G — Generic Retro/VHS Nostalgia

**What it looks like:**
- Tape lines
- Washed colors
- Old TV texture
- Soft blur
- Nostalgic media flavor
- But no identity distortion

**Why it's wrong:**
Retro media aesthetics are adjacent, but not sufficient.

**Common symptoms:**
- "Vintage" mood doing all the work
- No body fracture
- No emotional corruption logic
- Too cozy
- Too obvious

**Fix:**
Push the body/screen relationship:
- Body becomes signal
- Face becomes banded
- Captions obsessively repeat
- Time trails emerge
- Compression or text enters the portrait

**Replacement thought:**
Old media texture is seasoning, not the meal.

---

### DRIFT H — Analog Horror Cliché

**What it looks like:**
- Security camera look
- Creepy face
- Darkness
- Timecode
- Noise
- Spooky institutional vibe

**Why it's wrong:**
Glitchcore can overlap with this, but analog horror is usually narrative-creepy first. Your thing is broader, hotter, weirder, more emotional, more glamorous, more pop-capable.

**Common symptoms:**
- Too horror-coded
- Too sepulchral
- Too monochrome
- Too cinematic scare bait
- Not enough beauty / pop / synthetic pleasure

**Fix:**
Bring back:
- Palette energy
- Body glamour
- Text intimacy
- Candy or gloss branches
- Emotional ambiguity instead of straightforward creepiness

**Replacement thought:**
Unease is fine. Genre cliché is lazy.

---

### DRIFT I — Abstract Texture with No Human Stakes

**What it looks like:**
- Cool corruption
- Nice colors
- Nice layers
- But the body disappears as meaningful subject

**Why it's wrong:**
The style is about figure and identity distortion. Without that, it becomes pure abstract glitch.

**Common symptoms:**
- No readable face/pose/silhouette
- No subject retrieval path
- Textures dominate everything
- No emotional anchor

**Fix:**
Restore:
- Eyes
- Lips
- Silhouette
- Gesture
- Profile
- Repeated phrase tied to the subject
- One clear human zone

**Replacement thought:**
There should be a person in the machine damage somewhere.

---

### DRIFT J — Too Safe / Too Tasteful

**What it looks like:**
- Elegant
- Refined
- Nicely arranged
- Colors tasteful
- Effects restrained
- Everything balanced
- No real risk

**Why it's wrong:**
Glitchcore needs some wrongness. If it's too tasteful, it loses the electric little bastard in its soul.

**Common symptoms:**
- No aggression
- No excess
- No rupture
- No emotionally strange move
- Feels designed for approval

**Fix:**
Add one element that misbehaves:
- Uglier compression
- Repeated obsessive text
- Harsher chroma bruise
- Denser stack
- More temporal weirdness
- Tackier color intrusion
- Unresolved interface debris

**Replacement thought:**
Leave one nail crooked on purpose.

## 3. Positive Anchor Rules

### Rule 1 — Preserve a human retrieval path

There should usually be at least one:
- Eye
- Mouth
- Profile line
- Silhouette
- Shoulder line
- Hand gesture
- Head angle
- Emotional phrase tied to a body zone

Without retrieval, the style loses its teeth.

### Rule 2 — Let the artifact alter the subject

The distortion must change:
- Anatomy
- Identity
- Movement
- Emotional tone
- Material feel
- Screen relationship

If the figure underneath is untouched, the artifact is too superficial.

### Rule 3 — Use one lead corruption behavior

Even maximal images need a lead actor:
- Mainly channel split
- Mainly overprint
- Mainly compression chew
- Mainly text debris
- Mainly temporal echo

Then support it.

### Rule 4 — Let beauty survive through damage

The sweet spot is often:
- Attractive subject
- Unstable treatment
- Injury with glamour
- Pleasure contaminated by failure

Too clean is dead. Too ugly is dead in a different way.

### Rule 5 — Use color with hierarchy

Even wild palettes need:
- Dominant hues
- Collision hues
- Accent punctures
- Rest zones
- Saturation structure

Rainbow without architecture is a clown car wreck.

### Rule 6 — Make the media feel specific

Ask: What kind of failure is this?
- CRT?
- Terminal?
- CCTV?
- Codec damage?
- Repost mush?
- Subtitle smear?
- Lag trail?
- Screenshot fatigue?
- Phosphor striping?

Specificity keeps the style from turning generic.

### Rule 7 — Emotional weirdness matters

The image should feel like something happened to the self. Could be:
- Dreamy
- Erotic
- Lonely
- Bratty
- Haunted
- Panicked
- Glam
- Internet-broken
- Ecstatic

But it should not be emotionally blank.

- "Retro digital mood"

- "Synthwave face"

- "Y2K cyber edit"
- "Dreamy digital portrait"
- "Edgy internet girl"
- "Vaporwave face"
- "Cool broken screen vibe"

These aren't banned, but they're weak as hell on their own.

---

## 5. Better Replacement Language

Instead of vague bullshit, use specific statements like:
- "Channel-split face planes with magenta/cyan misregistration"
- "Repeated subtitle fragments smeared across the jawline"
- "Quantized bloom breaking skin gradients into visible tonal steps"
- "Ghost-frame hand trails lagging behind a glossy pose"
- "Scanline contour bands rebuilding the profile in luminous stripes"
- "Packet-loss bruises interrupting a candy-bright beauty image"
- "Overprinted facial copies drifting out of sync like delayed selfhood"
- "Terminal text behaving like low-opacity confession fog"
- "Chroma smear bleeding lipstick sideways into codec haze"
- "Sparkle-static and gloss bloom contaminating a diva-coded portrait"

That's the good shit.

---

## 6. Short Evaluation Questions

When checking an image, ask:
1. What is the main artifact driver?
2. What has actually happened to the body?
3. What kind of media failure does this imply?
4. What is the palette hierarchy?
5. What emotion does the corruption create?
6. What prevents this from being generic?
7. If I removed the artifacting, would the image still basically be the same?

If the answer to 7 is yes, you probably need more real corruption.

---

## 7. Summary Sentence

Anti-drift rules keep glitchcore specific by protecting artifact logic, body transformation, emotional consequence, palette hierarchy, and media-failure specificity from collapsing into generic cyberpunk, fake glitch filters, dead beauty edits, or random effect soup.

---

## 8. Short Anchor Rule

Don't decorate the portrait with glitch. Let glitch rewrite the portrait.
