# Palette Energy System

How color choices create emotional temperature in glitchcore.

## Purpose

This file defines how color palettes work in glitchcore — not just what colors to use, but how they create emotional temperature, energy levels, and aesthetic identity.

This is the part that helps the app stop going:
"use bright colors"
and start going:
"hot magenta dominant + cyan collision = hyperpop rupture energy."

The palette system here is designed to make color choices emotionally intelligent and stylistically specific.

---

## 1. Palette Architecture

### 1.1 The Three-Color Structure

Every glitchcore palette has three roles:

```
DOMINANT HUE FAMILY (60-70% of visual weight)
└── Sets emotional temperature
└── Defines the "mood" of the piece
└── Usually warm OR cool, not both

COLLISION HUE FAMILY (20-30% of visual weight)
└── Creates tension and energy
└── Complementary or contrasting to dominant
└── Provides the "spark" or "friction"

ACCENT PUNCTURE (5-10% of visual weight)
└── Sharp highlights
└── Usually white, black, or neon
└── Provides focal points and clarity
```

### 1.2 Palette Energy Formula

```
Energy = (Saturation × Contrast) + (Collision Strength) - (Mutedness)

Where:
- Saturation: 0-1 (color intensity)
- Contrast: 0-1 (difference between colors)
- Collision Strength: 0-1 (how much colors fight)
- Mutedness: 0-1 (how much colors are softened)
```

### 5.1 Seductive → Hyperpop Rupture

### 5.2 Bratty → Codec Candy Crash

**Why:** Saturated magenta + flat cyan = "I don't care but I'm beautiful" energy.

### 5.3 Dreamy → Pastel Signal Melt

**Why:** Soft pinks and lavenders create dissociative, memory-like atmosphere.

**Why:** Full spectrum with cyan-magenta dominance = maximum energy and instability.

### 5.5 Lonely → Phosphor Noir

**Why:** Cold teals with minimal warmth = isolation and institutional distance.

### 5.6 Glamorous → Gloss Electric Glam

### 5.7 Haunted → Phosphor Noir

### 5.8 Decadent → Rainbow Channel Hallucination

**Why:** Maximum color = maximum excess, beautiful overload.

## 6. Palette Modulation

### 6.1 Increasing Energy

```
+ Increase saturation
+ Increase contrast between dominant and collision
+ Add brighter accent
+ Reduce mutedness
```

### 6.2 Decreasing Energy

```
+ Decrease saturation
+ Reduce contrast
+ Soften accent
+ Increase mutedness
+ Add pastel tones
```

### 6.3 Shifting Temperature

```
Hot → Cool:
+ Shift dominant toward magenta → cyan
+ Reduce warm tones
+ Increase cool collision

Cool → Hot:
+ Shift dominant toward cyan → magenta
+ Add warm collision
+ Increase saturation
```

## 7. Palette Drift Detection

### 7.1 Rainbow Clown Soup

**Symptoms:** All colors equal, no hierarchy

**Fix:** Reduce to 3 colors, establish dominant

### 7.2 Muddy Browns

**Symptoms:** Colors mixing to brown/gray

**Fix:** Increase saturation, separate hues more

### 7.3 Too Cold

**Symptoms:** Emotionally distant, unengaging

**Fix:** Add warm collision or shift dominant warmer

### 7.4 Too Hot

**Symptoms:** Overwhelming, aggressive

**Fix:** Add cool collision, reduce saturation
